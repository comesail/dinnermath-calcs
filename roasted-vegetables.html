FAQs

How many roasted vegetables per person?
For a side, plan about 4 ounces cooked per adult or teen and about 3 ounces per child. For buffet or grazing, plan about 4 to 5 ounces per adult or teen and 3 to 4 ounces per child. For a vegetarian main, plan about 8 to 10 ounces per adult or teen and 6 to 8 ounces per child. Use Appetite, Event length, Leftovers, and Mix style to adjust.
What does Vegetable mix style do?
Mixed medley uses the baseline. Root heavy nudges portions up by about 10 percent since roots are denser and more filling. Light greens nudges portions down by about 10 percent since they are lighter.
How do appetite, event length, and leftovers work?
Appetite applies 0.90 Light, 1.00 Typical, or 1.20 Hearty. Event length applies 0.95, 1.00, 1.08, or 1.15. Leftovers adds 0 percent, +10 percent, or +20 percent to the final rounded amount.
How do you convert pounds to cups?
We convert cooked pounds to cups at about 2.5 cups per pound. This is a planning estimate, not a precise kitchen measure, and it varies by vegetable cut and roast level.
How is price calculated?
Subtotal equals rounded raw pounds times your unit price per pound. We then add sales tax and any fees to estimate a total and cost per person.

Quick notes

  • Baseline per person - cooked weight: Side about 4 oz adults or teens and 3 oz children. Vegetarian main about 8 to 10 oz adults or teens and 6 to 8 oz children.
  • Course multipliers: Side 1.00x, Buffet or grazing 1.15x, Vegetarian main 1.60x, Tasting plate 0.70x.
  • Mix style options: Mixed medley 1.00x, Root heavy 1.10x, Light greens 0.90x.
  • Cook loss and cups: We assume about 0.80 cooked yield from raw weight and about 2.5 cups per cooked pound.
  • Disclaimer: Estimates only; confirm with your grocer or caterer and adjust for your crowd.
