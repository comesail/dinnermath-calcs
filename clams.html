FAQs

How many clams per person?
As a main, plan about 12 clams per adult (about 10 for teens and 7–8 for children). For a mixed buffet, plan about 8 per adult. Appetizers are about 4 per adult.
How do you convert clams to pounds?
We multiply the needed clam count by your size (about 1.5–2.5 oz live each) and divide by 16 to estimate total live pounds. We round counts up to whole clams.
Buy by the dozen or by the clam?
Many markets price by the dozen. Enter a price per dozen or per clam; if both are set, we use the dozen price.
How much for 10, 25, 50, or 100 people?
For a main with typical appetite, rough guides are: 10 people about 120 clams, 25 people about 300, 50 people about 600, 100 people about 1,200. Use the calculator for your exact mix and size.
Does this account for shells and yield?
We plan by whole live clams and show live weight. If you want more meat per person, pick a larger size.

Quick notes

  • Per-adult baselines: Main about 12; Mixed about 8; Appetizer about 4. Teens ≈ 0.8 adult; Children ≈ 0.6 adult.
  • Size choices: Small about 1.5 oz live each; Medium about 2.0 oz; Large about 2.5 oz.
  • Rounding: We round the clam count up to whole clams; pounds shown are approximate.
  • Context multipliers: Appetite, event length, and leftovers can shift amounts by about 10–20%.
  • Disclaimer: Estimates only; confirm availability and size with your seafood market.
