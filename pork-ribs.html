FAQs

How many pounds of ribs per person?
Plan about 1.25 pounds raw baby backs per adult-equivalent guest. Spare ribs need closer to 1.5 pounds. Adjust for appetite, event length, and leftovers.
How many racks is that?
Baby back racks average 2.5–3 lb raw and serve ~2 adults. Spare racks are heavier (3.5–4 lb) and serve ~2–3 adults. The calculator outputs an approximate rack count.
Are ribs counted raw or cooked?
Weights are raw, uncooked. Cooked yield is lower (~65% of raw weight). The calculator already accounts for this in the per-person baseline.
What if ribs are just part of a buffet?
Reduce portion share by choosing “Light appetite” or shorten event length. The calculator scales weight down.
Should I add extra for leftovers?
Yes—pick “Some leftovers” (+7%) or “Extra leftovers” (+12%) to build in a buffer.
