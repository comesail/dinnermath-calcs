FAQs

How much pork ribs per person?
As a main dish, plan about 8 ounces cooked per adult (about 6.5 oz for teens and 4.8 oz for children). For a mixed buffet, plan about 6 ounces cooked per adult. Appetizers are about 3.5 ounces cooked per adult.
Baby back vs. St. Louis — does it change how much to buy?
Yes — bone and trim affect yield. We estimate raw pounds by dividing cooked ounces by a cut yield: Baby back ≈ 55% yield (about 45% shrink) and St. Louis/Spare ≈ 50% yield (about 50% shrink).
How do you calculate raw pounds to buy?
We estimate total cooked ounces from your guest mix and context (appetite, event length, leftovers), then divide by the cut’s yield to get raw pounds. We round to the nearest quarter-pound for easy shopping.
How much should I buy for 10, 25, 50, or 100 people?
For a main with typical appetite and baby back ribs, rough guides are: 10 people ≈ 8–9 lb raw, 25 people ≈ 20–23 lb, 50 people ≈ 40–46 lb, 100 people ≈ 80–92 lb. Use the calculator for your exact mix and settings.
Does the estimate include sides?
No—weights shown are for the ribs only. Add sides separately. If your menu is side-heavy, pick “Mixed buffet” to scale portions down.

Quick notes

  • Per-adult cooked baselines: Main about 8 oz; Mixed about 6 oz; Appetizer about 3.5 oz. Teens ≈ 0.8 adult; Children ≈ 0.6 adult.
