FAQs

How much whole lobster per person?
As a main dish, plan about 1.5 lb live weight per adult. For a mixed buffet, about 1.1 lb per adult. Appetizers are about 0.75 lb per adult. Teens trend between adults and children; children usually eat about 0.8× adult as a main.
What about edible yield?
Whole lobsters average about 20–25% edible meat (tail and claws). Our per-person baselines are set for live weight and already account for shell.
How many lobsters is that?
Choose a Lobster size preset (Chick 1.0 lb, Standard 1.25 lb, Large 2.0 lb, Jumbo 3.0 lb) or pick Custom to enter your own weight. We convert total live pounds into an about lobster count and round up.
Why include leftovers and appetite?
Appetite (Light/Typical/Hearty), event length, and leftovers (+10% or +20%) let you tune portions so you don’t run short.
How does pricing work?
Subtotal is rounded live pounds × price per lb. Then we add your tax and any fees to estimate a total and per-person cost.

Quick notes

  • Assumptions (live weight): Adults 1.5 lb; Teens 1.2 lb; Children 1.0 lb.
  • Course multipliers: Main 1.00; Mixed 0.75; Appetizer 0.50.
  • Context multipliers: Appetite (0.90 / 1.00 / 1.20); Event length (0.95 / 1.00 / 1.08 / 1.15); Leftovers (No / +10% / +20%).
  • Size presets (live): Chick ≈ 1.0 lb; Standard ≈ 1.25 lb; Large ≈ 2.0 lb; Jumbo ≈ 3.0 lb (or use Custom).
  • Count: About lobsters = rounded live pounds ÷ lobster size (rounded up).
  • Rounding: Live purchase rounded to the nearest quarter-pound for easy shopping; count rounded up.
  • Disclaimer: Estimates only; confirm with your seafood market and adjust for your crowd.
