Quick Notes

  • Baseline chops: 2–3 per adult-equivalent (choose in the dropdown).
  • Rack size: Standard racks are 8 bones; some are 7–9. The calculator converts bones → racks.
  • Weight per chop: Typical frenched chops average ~3.5 oz raw each (varies 3–4+ oz).

FAQs

How many chops per person?
Plan 2–3 lamb chops per adult-equivalent for a main course. Use the dropdown to match your service style.
How many racks do I need?
We divide your total chops by your selected bones per rack (usually 8) and round up to whole racks.
How do chops translate to pounds?
We multiply chops by your chosen raw ounces per bone and convert to pounds. Typical frenched chops are ~3.5 oz raw each.
Should I round up?
Yes—bone size and trim vary (frenched vs not). Rounding up avoids shortfalls.
