FAQs

How many beef ribs per person?
For plate ribs, plan about 1 bone per adult-equivalent. For back ribs, plan about 2 bones per adult-equivalent (they’re smaller and bonier).
How do bones translate to pounds?
Plate rib bones often weigh 1.5–2.5 lb each raw; back rib bones ~0.6–1.0 lb each raw. The calculator converts bones to total pounds using these averages.
What about mixed menus?
If ribs share the plate with other mains, choose Light appetite or shorten event length to reduce portions.
Should I round up?
Yes—bone sizes vary widely. Rounding up avoids running short.
