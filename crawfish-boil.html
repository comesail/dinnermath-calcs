FAQs

How many pounds of crawfish per person?
For a main boil, plan about 4 pounds live per adult (about 3.2 for teens and 2.4 for children). For a mixed buffet, plan about 3 pounds per adult. Appetizers are about 1.5 pounds per adult.
How much tail meat does that make?
Live crawfish yield about 15 percent tail meat by weight. We show an estimate based on that yield.
Do sides (corn, potatoes, sausage) change the amount?
Yes. Choose “Heavy extras” to scale the crawfish down slightly, or “Light extras” to scale up a bit.
How much for 10, 25, 50, or 100 people?
For a main with typical appetite: 10 people about 40 lb live, 25 people about 100 lb, 50 people about 200 lb, 100 people about 400 lb. Use the calculator for your exact mix and settings.
Are results live weight or cooked?
We plan by live weight to buy and also show an estimate of tail meat pounds. Prices are assumed per pound live.

Quick notes

  • Per-adult baselines (live): Main about 4 lb; Mixed about 3 lb; Appetizer about 1.5 lb. Teens about 0.8 adult; Children about 0.6 adult.
  • Yield guide: Tail meat is about 15 percent of live weight (varies by size and prep).
  • Extras: Heavy sides reduce crawfish needed; light sides increase it a little.
  • Rounding: We round live purchase to the nearest whole pound and show sacks at 30 lb each.
  • Disclaimer: Estimates only; confirm availability, size, and pricing with your market.
