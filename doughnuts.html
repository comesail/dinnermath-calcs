FAQs

How many doughnuts per person?
For breakfast or dessert, plan about 1.0 doughnut per adult, about 1.5 for teens, and about 0.5 for children. Coffee-break trays often run a bit lighter.
What does the size class do?
Sizes are expressed by baked diameter with an equivalent per-doughnut weight. For example, Small is about 3 in and about 2.5 oz each; Average about 3.5 in and about 3.0 oz; Large about 4 in and about 3.5 oz; Jumbo about 4.5 in and about 4.0 oz. We compute pounds from the per-piece weight.
Breakfast, dessert table, or coffee break — what changes?
Course mode multipliers: Breakfast 1.00x, Dessert table 0.85x, Coffee break 0.75x, Appetizer 0.50x. Combine with appetite, event length, and leftovers to fit your crowd.
How are dozens calculated?
We divide by 12 and round up to whole dozens. You can tweak appetite/leftovers to add a buffer for popular flavors.
How does pricing work here?
Choose your price mode: per dozen, per each, or per pound. We multiply your unit price by the rounded quantity in that mode (dozens, eaches, or pounds), then add your tax and fees to estimate totals.

Quick notes

  • Base portions: Adults about 1.0 doughnut; Teens about 1.5; Children about 0.5.
  • Size classes: Small about 3 in (about 2.5 oz each); Average about 3.5 in (about 3.0 oz); Large about 4 in (about 3.5 oz); Jumbo about 4.5 in (about 4.0 oz).
  • Course multipliers: Breakfast 1.00x; Dessert 0.85x; Coffee break 0.75x; Appetizer 0.50x.
  • Context multipliers: Appetite (0.90 / 1.00 / 1.20); Event length (0.95 / 1.00 / 1.08 / 1.15); Leftovers (No / +10% / +20%).
  • Rounding: Dozens round up to whole dozens. Doughnut pounds round to the nearest 1/4 lb for pricing.
  • Disclaimer: Estimates only; confirm with your grocer/bakery and adjust for your crowd.
