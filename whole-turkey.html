FAQs

How much whole turkey per person?
As a main protein, plan about 1.25 lb per adult, about 1.5 lb per teen, and about 0.75 lb per child raw. Bones and moisture loss mean only part becomes edible meat.
What do the turkey class labels mean?
Small about 12 lb, Average about 14 lb, Large about 16 lb, Jumbo about 20 lb per bird. We use these to estimate about how many whole turkeys for your raw-pound target.
Main protein, mixed buffet, or sandwiches — what changes?
Course mode multipliers: Main 1.00x, Mixed buffet 0.75x, Sandwich station 0.80x, Appetizer 0.50x. Combine with appetite, event length, and leftovers to fit your crowd.
How does pricing work here?
Subtotal = rounded raw pounds × unit price per pound. Then we add your tax and any extra fees to estimate a total and per-person cost.
How many pounds of cooked meat does that yield?
A whole turkey typically yields about 40 to 50 percent cooked, boneless meat. If you need a cooked-meat target (for sandwiches), multiply the cooked pounds you want by about 2.2 to 2.5 to get raw whole-turkey pounds. This calculator already factors that shrinkage into the raw-pound results you see.

Quick notes

  • Base portions (raw lb per person): Adults about 1.25 lb; Teens about 1.50 lb; Children about 0.75 lb.
  • Course multipliers: Main 1.00x; Mixed 0.75x; Sandwich 0.80x; Appetizer 0.50x.
