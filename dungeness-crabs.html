FAQs

How much Dungeness crab per person?
As a main, plan about 2.0 pounds per adult (whole, in shell). Teens count about three-quarters of an adult and children about half.
How do you estimate the whole-crab count?
We divide the rounded raw pounds by your average whole-crab weight (default about 2.0 lb each) to get an approximate count.
What if I need picked crab meat only?
This tool sizes whole crabs in shell. For meat only, assume about 20–25 percent edible yield from whole crab and increase the raw pounds accordingly.
How do leftovers and appetite change amounts?
Pick Light, Typical, or Hearty appetite, and optionally add +10 percent or +20 percent leftovers. Both settings scale the total you should buy.
Are the costs exact?
No—these are planning estimates. Subtotal uses rounded raw pounds × price, then adds your sales tax and any extra fees.

Quick notes

  • Baseline: about 2.0 lb per adult (whole, in shell). Teens count about 0.75 of an adult; children about 0.50.
  • Average crab weight: default about 2.0 lb each—edit this to match your supplier.
  • Leftovers: add about +10 percent or +20 percent if you want extra servings.
  • Rounding: raw purchase is rounded to the nearest quarter-pound for easy shopping.
  • Pricing: cost = rounded raw pounds × price + sales tax + extra fees; planning estimate only.
