FAQs

How many Dungeness crabs per person?
For a main dish, plan about 1 crab per adult at an average 2.0 lb each. For a mixed buffet, about ¾ crab per adult. For appetizers, about ½ crab per adult. Teens trend between adults and children; children usually eat about half an adult portion.
Are these amounts for whole, in-shell crabs?
Yes — this tool estimates raw pounds for whole Dungeness crabs. Results already account for shell weight.
What if my crabs are larger or smaller than 2.0 lb?
Adjust Crab size each (lb). We convert total raw pounds into an about count using your size.
Why include leftovers and appetite?
Appetite (Light/Typical/Hearty), event length, and leftovers (+10% or +20%) let you tune portions so you don’t run short.
How does pricing work?
Subtotal is rounded raw pounds × price per lb. Then we add your tax and any fees to estimate a total and per-person cost.

Quick notes

  • Assumptions (raw, whole): Adults 2.0 lb; Teens 1.5 lb; Children 1.0 lb.
  • Course multipliers: Main 1.00; Mixed 0.75; Appetizer 0.50.
  • Context multipliers: Appetite (0.90 / 1.00 / 1.20); Event length (0.95 / 1.00 / 1.08 / 1.15); Leftovers (No / +10% / +20%).
  • Rounding: Raw purchase rounded to the nearest quarter-pound for easy shopping.
  • Count: About how many crabs = rounded raw pounds ÷ your crab size.
  • Disclaimer: Estimates only; confirm with your seafood market/fishmonger and adjust for your crowd.
