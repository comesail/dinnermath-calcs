FAQs

How much fruit per person?
As a side, plan about 6 oz per adult, about 7 oz for teens, and about 4 oz for children. Entree portions are heavier; tasting flights are lighter. Appetite, event length, and leftovers adjust the estimate.
Fruit salad vs fruit tray — what changes?
Fruit salad shows cups and tubs; fruit tray uses pounds and trays. The total fruit estimate is the same for both, but the packaging units differ.
What do the package and tray sizes mean?
Fruit salad tubs are common retail sizes: 16 oz, 32 oz, and 48 oz. Fruit trays are common platters and pans: round 12 inch about 5 lb, round 16 inch about 8 lb, half steam pan 12x10x2.5 in about 12 lb, full steam pan 20x12x2.5 in about 20 lb.
How does pricing work here?
Subtotal equals the unit price applied to the rounded quantity for your mode: per tub, per pound, or per tray. Then we add your tax and any extra fees to estimate total and per-person cost.
Do you round up?
Tubs and trays round up to whole units; pounds display to one decimal; cups are whole numbers. Use leftovers of plus 10 percent or plus 20 percent for buffer.

Quick notes

  • Base per-person (side): Adults about 6 oz; Teens about 7 oz; Children about 4 oz.
  • Course multipliers: Side 1.00x; Entree 1.60x; Party 1.20x; Tasting 0.70x.
  • Leftovers options: No; plus 10 percent; plus 20 percent.
  • Rounding: Tubs and trays to whole units; pounds to one decimal; cups to whole numbers.
  • Disclaimer: Estimates only; confirm with your grocer or caterer and adjust for your crowd.
