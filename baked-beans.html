Questions and answers about baked beans per person

How much baked beans per person?
As a side, plan about three quarters of a cup per adult. Teens count as about three quarters of an adult; children count as about one half. As a hearty main, plan about one and one quarter cups per adult. The calculator adjusts for appetite, event length, and leftovers.
How many cups or pounds of baked beans for 10 people?
About 7.5 cups (about 3.8 pounds). Adjust up or down by ten to twenty percent for appetite and guest mix.
How many cups or pounds of baked beans for 25 people?
About 18.75 cups (about 9.4 pounds). Use the preset and tweak Adults, Teens, or Children if needed.
How many cups or pounds of baked beans for 50 people?
About 37.5 cups (about 18.8 pounds). Use the calculator for your exact mix and appetite.
How many cups or pounds of baked beans for 100 people?
About 75 cups (about 37.5 pounds). Consider leftovers and serving style.
Do you assume shrinkage while warming?
Shrinkage is negligible because beans are saucy. We treat cups as served volume. Pounds are based on cups multiplied by weight per cup (default is eight ounces per cup, adjustable with Style or Density).
How do I budget total cost?
Enter a price per pound, sales tax percent, and any fixed fees. We calculate subtotal, tax and fees, total, and cost per person automatically.

Quick notes

  • Baselines (cups per adult): Side about 0.75; Buffet about 0.60; Main about 1.25.
  • Guest weighting: Teens are about 0.75 of an adult; Children are about 0.5 of an adult.
  • Density: One cup is about eight ounces (one half pound). “Thick or loaded” adds about five percent; “Light or saucy” reduces about five percent.
  • Context: Appetite, event length, and leftovers can shift totals by ten to twenty percent.
  • Disclaimer: Estimates only; confirm with your caterer or product labels.
