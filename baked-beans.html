Questions & answers about baked beans per person

How much baked beans per person?
As a side, plan about ¾ cup per adult. Teens count ~¾ of an adult; children ~½. As a hearty main, plan about 1¼ cups per adult. The calculator adjusts for appetite, event length, and leftovers.
How many cups/pounds of baked beans for 10 people?
About 7.5 cups (≈ 3.8 lb). Adjust ±10–20% for appetite and guest mix.
How many cups/pounds of baked beans for 25 people?
About 18.75 cups (≈ 9.4 lb). Tap the preset and tweak Adults/Teens/Children if needed.
How many cups/pounds of baked beans for 50 people?
About 37.5 cups (≈ 18.8 lb). Use the calculator for your exact mix and appetite.
How many cups/pounds of baked beans for 100 people?
About 75 cups (≈ 37.5 lb). Consider leftovers and serving style.
Do you assume shrinkage while warming?
Negligible; beans are saucy. We treat cups as served volume. Pounds are based on cups × weight per cup (defaults to 8 oz/cup, tweakable via Style/Density).
How do I budget total cost?
Enter a price per pound, tax %, and any fixed fees. We’ll calculate subtotal, tax+fees, total, and cost per person automatically.

Quick notes

  • Baselines (cups/adult): Side ≈ 0.75; Buffet ≈ 0.6; Main ≈ 1.25.
  • Guest weighting: Teens ≈ 0.75 adult; Children ≈ 0.5 adult.
  • Density: 1 cup ≈ 8 oz (0.5 lb). Thick/loaded adds ~5%; light/saucy reduces ~5%.
  • Context multipliers: Appetite, event length, leftovers can shift totals ±10–20%.
  • Disclaimer: Estimates only; confirm with your caterer or product labels.
