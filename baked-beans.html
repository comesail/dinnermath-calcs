FAQs

How much baked beans per person?
As a main, plan about 12 ounces per adult. For a mixed buffet, about 8 ounces per adult. Appetizers are about 4 ounces per adult. Teens trend between adults and children; children usually eat about 60% of an adult portion.
Are these amounts cooked or raw?
These amounts are for cooked, ready-to-serve baked beans (homemade or canned). No extra shrink factor is applied.
How do appetite and event length change amounts?
Hearty groups and longer events eat more. Appetite, event length, and leftovers (+10% or +20%) scale totals up or down.
How is cost calculated?
Subtotal is rounded pounds × price per lb. Then we add your tax and any fees to estimate a total and per-person cost.
Does the estimate include sides?
No — weights shown are for baked beans only. Add other sides separately. If your menu is side-heavy, pick “Mixed buffet.”

Quick notes

  • Assumptions (ready-to-serve): Adults 12 oz; Teens 9.6 oz; Children 7.2 oz.
  • Course multipliers: Main 1.00; Mixed 0.67; Appetizer 0.33.
  • Context multipliers: Appetite (0.90 / 1.00 / 1.20); Event length (0.95 / 1.00 / 1.08 / 1.15); Leftovers (No / +10% / +20%).
  • Units: We calculate in ounces per person, then convert to pounds.
  • Rounding: Purchase rounded to the nearest quarter-pound for easy shopping.
  • Disclaimer: Estimates only; confirm with your grocer/caterer and adjust for your crowd.
