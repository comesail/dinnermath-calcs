FAQs

How much baked beans per person?
As a side, plan about 1/2 cup (about 4 oz) per adult, about 5 oz for teens, and about 3 oz for children. BBQ plates tend to run a little heavier.
What do the can sizes mean?
We label common sizes as Small (about 8 oz), Average (about 15 oz), Large (about 28 oz), and Jumbo (#10 can about 108 oz). The calculator converts needed ounces into about how many cans.
Homemade vs premade — what changes?
Homemade uses your pot/pan yield and outputs about pounds. Premade (canned) lets you pick a can size and shows about cans needed. Appetite, event length, and leftovers still adjust the estimates either way.
How does pricing work here?
Subtotal = unit price applied to the rounded quantity for your mode: per can (rounded cans × price per can) or per pound (rounded pounds × price per pound). Then we add your tax and any extra fees to estimate a total and per-person cost.
Do you round up?
Pounds round to the nearest 1/4 lb for pricing; cans round up to whole cans. You can add +10% or +20% leftovers if you want extra buffer.

Quick notes

  • Base portions (as side): Adults ~ 4 oz; Teens ~ 5 oz; Children ~ 3 oz.
  • Course multipliers: Side 1.00x; BBQ plate 1.20x; Tasting 0.75x.
  • Canned size classes: Small 8 oz; Average 15 oz; Large 28 oz; Jumbo #10 can 108 oz.
  • Context multipliers: Appetite (0.90 / 1.00 / 1.20); Event length (0.95 / 1.00 / 1.08 / 1.15); Leftovers (No / +10% / +20%).
  • Rounding: Pounds to nearest 1/4 lb; cans to whole cans.
  • Disclaimer: Estimates only; confirm with your grocer/caterer and adjust for your crowd.
