FAQs

How much lobster per person?
As a main dish, plan about 0.60 lb in-shell tails per adult. For a mixed buffet, about 0.45 lb per adult. Appetizers are about 0.30 lb per adult. Teens trend between adults and children; children usually eat about 60% of an adult portion.
Are these amounts for tails in shell?
Yes — this tool estimates shell-on lobster tails (typically pre-cooked or raw frozen). The per-person baselines already account for shell weight.
How many tails is that?
Choose a Tail size preset (Small 4 oz, Medium 6 oz, Large 8 oz, Jumbo 10 oz) or pick Custom to enter your own weight. We convert total raw pounds into an about tail count and round up.
Why include leftovers and appetite?
Appetite (Light/Typical/Hearty), event length, and leftovers (+10% or +20%) let you tune portions so you don’t run short.
How does pricing work?
Subtotal is rounded raw pounds × price per lb. Then we add your tax and any fees to estimate a total and per-person cost.

Quick notes

  • Assumptions (in-shell tails): Adults 0.60 lb; Teens 0.48 lb; Children 0.36 lb.
  • Course multipliers: Main 1.00; Mixed 0.75; Appetizer 0.50.
  • Context multipliers: Appetite (0.90 / 1.00 / 1.20); Event length (0.95 / 1.00 / 1.08 / 1.15); Leftovers (No / +10% / +20%).
  • Tail size presets: Small ≈ 0.25 lb (4 oz); Medium ≈ 0.375 lb (6 oz); Large ≈ 0.5 lb (8 oz); Jumbo ≈ 0.625 lb (10 oz) — or use Custom.
  • Tail count: About tails = rounded raw pounds ÷ tail size (rounded up).
  • Rounding: Raw purchase rounded to the nearest quarter-pound for easy shopping; tail count rounded up.
  • Disclaimer: Estimates only; confirm with your seafood market and adjust for your crowd.
