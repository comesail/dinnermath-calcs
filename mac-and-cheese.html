FAQs

How much mac and cheese per person?
As a side, plan about 6 oz per adult, about 7 oz for teens, and about 4 oz for children. Entree portions are heavier; tasting flights are lighter. Appetite, event length, and leftovers adjust the estimate.
Baked vs creamy — what changes?
We apply a small style tweak: Baked runs about 1.10x the base (denser), while Creamy uses the baseline.
What do the pan sizes mean?
We list common pans by dimensions with an about cooked weight they hold comfortably: 8x8 inch about 2.5 lb; 9x13 inch about 5 lb; half steam pan 12x10x2.5 in about 10 lb; full steam pan 20x12x2.5 in about 20 lb. We convert total pounds into about how many pans using the size you select.
How does pricing work here?
Subtotal equals the unit price applied to the rounded quantity for your mode: per pound (rounded pounds times price per pound) or per pan (rounded pans times price per pan). Then we add your tax and any extra fees to estimate total and per-person cost.
Do you round up?
Pans round up to whole pans; pounds display to one decimal; ounces display as whole numbers. Use leftovers of plus 10 percent or plus 20 percent for buffer.

Quick notes

  • Base per-person (side): Adults about 6 oz; Teens about 7 oz; Children about 4 oz.
  • Course multipliers: Side 1.00x; Entree 1.60x; Party 1.20x; Tasting 0.70x.
