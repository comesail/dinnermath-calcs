FAQs

How much coleslaw per person?
As a side at a mixed buffet, plan about 3 ounces per adult (about 2.4 oz for teens and 1.8 oz for children). As a main alongside BBQ plates, plan closer to 4 ounces per adult. Appetizers are about 2 ounces per adult.
Is coleslaw measured in raw or cooked weight?
Coleslaw is ready-to-eat — we estimate in raw pounds (no shrink). We round to the nearest quarter-pound for easy shopping.
Can I buy tubs instead of pounds?
Yes. Pick Store-bought (by the tub) and choose a Small (about 1 lb), Medium (about 2 lb), or Party (about 5 lb) tub. We convert pounds to an about tub count automatically.
Why include leftovers and appetite?
Appetite (Light/Typical/Hearty), event length, and leftovers (+10% or +20%) let you tune portions so you do not run short.
How does pricing work?
Subtotal is rounded raw pounds × price per lb. Then we add your tax and any fees to estimate a total and per-person cost.

Quick notes

  • Per-adult baselines (ounces, ready-to-eat): Mixed buffet about 3.0; Main plate about 4.0; Appetizer about 2.0. Teens ≈ 0.8 adult; Children ≈ 0.6 adult.
  • Course multipliers: Main 1.33; Mixed 1.00; Appetizer 0.67.
  • Context multipliers: Appetite (0.90 / 1.00 / 1.20); Event length (0.95 / 1.00 / 1.08 / 1.15); Leftovers (No / +10% / +20%).
  • Rounding: Raw purchase rounded to the nearest quarter-pound for easy shopping.
  • Count: About tubs = rounded raw pounds ÷ your tub size.
  • Disclaimer: Estimates only; confirm with your deli or grocer and adjust for your crowd.
