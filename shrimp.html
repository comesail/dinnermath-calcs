FAQs

How many pounds of shrimp per person?
For a shrimp main, plan roughly 8–12 ounces per adult (about ½–¾ pound raw). For appetizers, plan 3–5 ounces per adult. The calculator adjusts for appetite, event length, and leftovers.
What does “16–20 per pound” or “U10” mean?
Those are counts per pound. “16–20” means ~16 to 20 shrimp weigh one pound. “U10” means under 10 per pound (very large). We use the midpoint of your chosen range in the math.
Do shells change the amount I should buy?
Yes. Shell-on adds weight. If you are buying shell-on but serving peeled, consider turning on a small leftovers buffer to ensure enough edible shrimp.
How many pieces per person is that?
It depends on size. For 16–20 per pound, ½ lb is ~8–10 pieces; ¾ lb is ~12–15. The results show an approximate count for your chosen size.
Can I mix sizes or brands?
Yes. If sizes vary, pick the closest posted range. For mixed bags, choose an average (for example, 16–20 if your mix is 15–22).
Are results raw or cooked weight?
Results are raw purchase weight. Cooking loss depends on shell status and method, so finished yield will vary.
General note: sizes and pricing vary by season and region; confirm with your seafood market.
