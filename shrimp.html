Frequently Asked Questions

How many pounds of shrimp per person?

For shrimp as a main, plan roughly 8–12 ounces per adult (about ½–¾ pound raw). For appetizers, plan 3–5 ounces per adult. The calculator adjusts for appetite, event length, serving style, and whether you want leftovers.

What do “16–20 per pound” or “U10” mean?

They’re counts per pound. “16–20” means about 16 to 20 shrimp weigh one pound. “U10” means “under 10 per pound,” which are very large shrimp. We use the midpoint of the size range for the math so the piece counts are easy to read.

Shell-on vs. peeled—does it change the amount?

Yes. Shells add weight and reduce the edible portion. If you’re buying shell-on but serving peeled, consider turning on a small leftovers/buffer to avoid running short.

How many pieces is that per person?

It depends on size. For 16–20 per pound, ½ pound is about 8–10 shrimp; ¾ pound is roughly 12–15. The calculator shows an approximate piece count for the size you select.

Can I mix sizes or brands?

Yes. If sizes vary, choose the closest posted range at your store. For mixed sizes, pick an average (for example, choose 16–20 if your mix falls around 15–22 per pound).

Are results raw or cooked weight?

The recommendations are raw purchase weight. Actual cooked yield varies with shells, cooking method, and moisture loss.
