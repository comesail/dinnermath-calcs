FAQs

How many sheet cake slices per person?
Plan about 1 slice per adult, about 1.25 for teens, and about 0.5 for children as a dessert.
What do the sheet pan sizes mean?
Quarter sheet 9x13 in ~ 24 slices (2x2 in portions); Half sheet 12x18 in ~ 48 slices; Full sheet 18x24 in ~ 96 slices. We use these to convert needed slices to about how many cakes.
Dessert table, party main dessert, or coffee break — what changes?
Course multipliers: Dessert 1.00x, Party main dessert 1.20x, Coffee break 0.75x, Wedding slivers 0.65x. Combine with appetite, event length, and leftovers to fit your crowd.
How are cake pounds estimated?
We assume about 4.0 oz per dessert slice on average (party servings more, coffee-break slivers less), and convert slices to cake pounds for pricing. Pounds round to the nearest 1/4 lb.
How does pricing work here?
Subtotal = rounded cake pounds × unit price per pound. Then we add your tax and any extra fees to estimate a total and per-person cost. If your bakery prices per sheet or per slice, enter an equivalent per-pound price from your quote.

Quick notes

  • Base portions (by slice): Adults about 1; Teens about 1.25; Children about 0.5.
  • Course multipliers: Dessert 1.00x; Party main dessert 1.20x; Coffee 0.75x; Wedding slivers 0.65x.
  • Sheet pan sizes (approx slices): Quarter 9x13 in ~ 24; Half 12x18 in ~ 48; Full 18x24 in ~ 96.
  • Context multipliers: Appetite (0.90 / 1.00 / 1.20); Event length (0.95 / 1.00 / 1.08 / 1.15); Leftovers (No / +10% / +20%).
  • Rounding: Cakes round up to whole cakes. Cake pounds round to the nearest 1/4 lb.
  • Disclaimer: Estimates only; confirm with your bakery/caterer and adjust for your crowd.
