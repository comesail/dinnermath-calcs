FAQs

How many pie slices per person?
Plan about 1 slice per adult, about 1.25 for teens, and about 0.5 for children as a dessert. Wedding slivers run lighter; party main-dessert servings run heavier.
What do the pie size classes mean?
Small 8 in ~ 6 slices; Average 9 in ~ 8 slices; Large 10 in ~ 10 slices; Jumbo 12 in ~ 12 slices. We use these to convert needed slices to about how many pies.
Dessert table, wedding slivers, or party main dessert — what changes?
Course multipliers: Dessert 1.00x, Wedding slivers 0.65x, Party main dessert 1.20x, Coffee break 0.75x. Combine with appetite, event length, and leftovers to fit your crowd.
How are pie pounds estimated?
We assume about 5.0 oz per dessert slice on average (wedding slivers less, party servings more), and convert slices to pie pounds for pricing. Pounds round to the nearest 1/4 lb.
How does pricing work here?
Subtotal = unit price applied to the rounded quantity for your mode: per slice (rounded slices × price per slice) or per pie (rounded pies × price per pie). Then we add your tax and any extra fees to estimate a total and per-person cost.

Quick notes

  • Base portions (by slice): Adults about 1; Teens about 1.25; Children about 0.5.
  • Course multipliers: Dessert 1.00x; Wedding slivers 0.65x; Party main dessert 1.20x; Coffee 0.75x.
  • Pie size classes (approx slices): Small 8 in about 6; Average 9 in about 8; Large 10 in about 10; Jumbo 12 in about 12.
  • Context multipliers: Appetite (0.90 / 1.00 / 1.20); Event length (0.95 / 1.00 / 1.08 / 1.15); Leftovers (No / +10% / +20%).
  • Rounding: Pies round up to whole pies. Pie pounds round to the nearest 1/4 lb.
  • Disclaimer: Estimates only; confirm with your bakery/caterer and adjust for your crowd.
