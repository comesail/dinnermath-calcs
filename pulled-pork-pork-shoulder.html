FAQs

How much pulled pork per person?
Plan about 6 oz cooked per adult, about 8 oz cooked per teen, and about 4 oz cooked per child. We convert to raw pork shoulder using a typical cooked-yield assumption.
What does the shoulder class mean?
Typical whole shoulder weights: Small about 6 lb, Average about 8 lb, Large about 10 lb, Jumbo about 14 lb. We use this to estimate about how many shoulders to buy.
Main protein, mixed buffet, or sliders — what changes?
Course multipliers: Main 1.00x, Mixed buffet 0.75x, Sliders/Sandwiches 0.85x, Appetizer 0.50x. Combine with appetite, event length, and leftovers to fit your crowd.
What cooked-yield is assumed?
We assume about 62 percent yield from raw shoulder to cooked, boneless pulled pork after trimming and moisture loss. You can mentally adjust up or down a few percent based on fat content and cooking method.
How does pricing work here?
Subtotal = rounded raw pounds × unit price per pound. Then we add your tax and any extra fees to estimate a total and per-person cost.

Quick notes

  • Base portions (cooked): Adults about 6 oz; Teens about 8 oz; Children about 4 oz.
  • Yield assumption: About 62 percent from raw shoulder to cooked pulled pork.
  • Course multipliers: Main 1.00x; Mixed 0.75x; Sliders 0.85x; Appetizer 0.50x.
  • Context multipliers: Appetite (0.90 / 1.00 / 1.20); Event length (0.95 / 1.00 / 1.08 / 1.15); Leftovers (No / +10% / +20%).
  • Rounding: Shoulders round up to whole shoulders. Raw pounds round to the nearest 1/4 lb.
  • Disclaimer: Estimates only; confirm with your grocer/caterer and adjust for your crowd.
