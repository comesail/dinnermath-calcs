Questions and answers about chips and dip per person

How much chips and dip per person?
For light snacks, plan about one and a half ounces of chips and one ounce of dip per adult. For mixed parties, plan about two and a half ounces of chips and one and a half ounces of dip. For heavy game nights, plan about four ounces of chips and two ounces of dip per adult. Teens count as about three quarters of an adult; children count as about one half. Adjust for appetite, event length, and leftovers.
How many bags and cups of dip for 10 people?
With a 12 ounce bag and “mixed party,” expect roughly 2 bags of chips and about 2 cups of dip. Use the calculator for your exact mix and appetite.
How many for 25, 50, 100 people?
About 5–6 bags/4–5 cups for 25, 10–11 bags/8–9 cups for 50, and 20–21 bags/16–18 cups for 100 (mixed party baseline). Heavier game nights need more.
What bag size do you assume?
Default is a 12 ounce bag. Change in Inputs; bag count updates automatically.
How do I budget total cost?
Enter a price per bag for chips. For dip, choose price per jar (jar size in Inputs) or homemade (price per ounce). We calculate subtotal, tax and fees, total, and cost per person.

Quick notes

  • Per-adult baselines: Light ≈ 1.5 oz chips + 1.0 oz dip; Mixed ≈ 2.5 oz + 1.5 oz; Heavy ≈ 4.0 oz + 2.0 oz.
  • Guest weights: Teens ≈ 0.75 adult; Children ≈ 0.5 adult.
  • Context multipliers: Appetite, event length, and leftovers can shift totals ~10–20%.
  • Dip style: Thick/loaded adds ~10% dip; light/thin reduces ~10% (chips unchanged).
  • Packages: Chips round to whole bags. Dip rounds to quarter-cups; you can change the “ounces per cup”.
  • Pricing: Use price per jar with the jar size you selected in Inputs, or homemade price per ounce.
  • Disclaimer: Estimates only; confirm with your store sizes and your crowd.
