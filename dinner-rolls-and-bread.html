FAQs

How many rolls or bread slices per person?
Side with meal - about 1.5 pieces per adult or teen and about 1 piece per child. Bread basket or buffet - about 2 per adult or teen and 1 to 1.5 per child. Sandwich bar - plan about 2 to 3 rolls per person or about 2 sandwiches, which is about 4 slices per person. Tasting - about 1 each. Adjust with Appetite, Event length, and Leftovers.
What do Roll sizes and Loaf packs mean?
Roll sizes use diameter guidance: Small about 2.5 inches, Average about 3.0 inches, Large about 3.5 inches, Jumbo about 4.0 inches. Loaf packs describe typical retail loaves by slices per loaf with length and thickness hints: Standard about 20 slices (9 to 10 inch, medium), Small about 16 slices (about 8 inch, medium), Family or long about 24 slices (11 to 12 inch, thin), Texas toast about 12 slices (thick).
How do the multipliers work?
Appetite applies 0.90 Light, 1.00 Typical, or 1.20 Hearty. Event length applies 0.95, 1.00, 1.08, or 1.15. Leftovers adds 0 percent, +10 percent, or +20 percent to the final rounded amount.
How does pricing work?
Per piece uses rounded rolls or slices. Per pack uses dozens for rolls or loaves for sliced bread. We then add your sales tax and any fees to estimate a total and cost per person.
Do you round up?
We round to whole pieces and whole packs and show results as about.
