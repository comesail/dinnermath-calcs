FAQs

How much salad dressing per person?
Side salad about 1.5 oz per adult, about 1.75 oz for teens, and about 1.0 oz for children. Entree salads run heavier; tasting flights run lighter. We also nudge for style (vinaigrette vs creamy vs thick).
Homemade vs bottled — what changes?
Bottled lets you pick a common bottle size (8, 12, 16, 32 oz) and shows about bottles needed. Homemade focuses on total ounces, cups, and quarts for batching.
How do the style options affect portions?
Vinaigrette or thin about 0.90x, Creamy baseline 1.00x, Thick or chunky about 1.20x. This multiplies the base per-person needs.
How does pricing work here?
Subtotal = unit price applied to the rounded quantity for your mode: per bottle (rounded bottles × price per bottle) or per ounce (rounded ounces × price per ounce). Then we add your tax and any extra fees to estimate a total and per-person cost.
Do you round up?
Bottles round up to whole bottles. Ounces round to the nearest whole ounce for display; cups and quarts are shown as about values. Use leftovers +10 percent or +20 percent if you want extra buffer.

Quick notes

  • Base per-person (side salad): Adults 1.5 oz; Teens 1.75 oz; Children 1.0 oz.
  • Course multipliers: Side 1.00x; Entree 1.60x; Dip 1.30x; Tasting 0.70x.
  • Style multipliers: Vinaigrette 0.90x; Creamy 1.00x; Thick 1.20x.
  • Leftovers options: No, plus 10 percent, plus 20 percent.
  • Disclaimer: Estimates only; confirm with your grocer or caterer and adjust for your crowd.
