How many whole chickens per person?
For a main course with average appetite, plan about one whole 4.0-pound chicken for 3 adult-equivalent guests. The calculator scales this by appetite, event length, variety, and leftovers.

What if my chickens are smaller or larger?
Choose the closest size (3.5, 4.0, or 4.5 pounds). The tool adjusts servings per bird—larger birds serve slightly more.

Does this include sides and appetizers?
Yes. If you are serving many sides or multiple mains, change “Menu variety” to reduce the chicken share. For a light appetizer-style tasting, also set appetite to “Light.”

Can I mix whole chickens with pieces?
Yes. Use this tool for the whole birds and a separate tool for pieces, or estimate pieces as additional mains and reduce menu variety here.

Is the weight raw or cooked?
The size selector uses raw weight. The servings model accounts for typical cooked yield from whole birds.

How do I budget?
Enter your price per pound (plus tax/fees if needed). The tool returns estimated total and per-person costs.

Leftovers—how much should I add?
“Some leftovers” adds about seven percent. “Extra leftovers” adds about twelve percent.

Notes: This tool estimates quantities only—no cooking advice or food safety guidance. Adjust for your guests and menu.
