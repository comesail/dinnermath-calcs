FAQs

How much cooked chicken per person?
As an entree, plan about 6 oz per adult, about 8 oz for teens, and about 4 oz for children. Side or tasting portions are lighter. Appetite, event length, and leftovers adjust the estimate.
What does the bird size mean?
This is the average raw weight of the whole chickens you plan to buy. Options: Small about 3.0 lb, Average about 4.0 lb, Large about 5.0 lb, Jumbo about 6.0 lb. We convert raw pounds into about how many birds using your selection.
Do you account for bones and shrinkage?
Yes. We convert target cooked ounces to raw pounds using a typical whole chicken yield. The results already consider bones and moisture loss so shrinkage is included.
How does pricing work here?
Subtotal equals the unit price applied to the rounded quantity for your mode: per pound or per bird. Then tax and any extra fees are added for a total and per-person estimate.
Do you round up?
Birds round up to whole birds; pounds display to one decimal; cooked ounces display as whole numbers. You can add leftovers of plus 10 percent or plus 20 percent for buffer.

Quick notes

  • Base per-person (entree): Adults about 6 oz cooked; Teens about 8 oz; Children about 4 oz.
  • Course multipliers: Side 1.00x; Entree 1.60x; Party 1.20x; Tasting 0.70x.
  • Yield assumption: About 60 percent cooked meat from raw whole chicken.
  • Leftovers options: No; plus 10 percent; plus 20 percent.
  • Disclaimer: Estimates only; confirm with your grocer or caterer and adjust for your crowd.
