Questions & answers about corned beef per person

How much corned beef per person?
For a main dish, plan about ¾ lb raw per adult. Teens count ~¾ of an adult; children ~½. The calculator adjusts for appetite, event length, and leftovers.
How many pounds of corned beef for 10 people?
About 7.5 lb raw (≈ 5.0 lb cooked flat cut). Adjust ±10–20% for appetite and the mix of teens/children.
How many pounds of corned beef for 25 people?
About 18.75 lb raw (≈ 12.6 lb cooked flat cut). Tap the preset and tweak your guest mix if needed.
How many pounds of corned beef for 50 people?
About 37.5 lb raw (≈ 25.1 lb cooked flat cut). Use the calculator for your exact mix and appetite.
How many pounds of corned beef for 100 people?
About 75 lb raw (≈ 50.3 lb cooked flat cut). Consider leftovers and serving style.
What’s the cooked yield from corned beef brisket?
Flat cut loses ~33% during cooking; point loses ~36%. A 15 lb raw purchase yields roughly 10–10.5 lb cooked (flat) or slightly less (point).
Flat vs point — which should I buy?
Flat is leaner and slices evenly; point is fattier and extra flavorful but shrinks more. Choose flat for tidy slices, point for juicy shreddy sandwiches.
Serving sandwiches vs plated — does it change the math?
Yes. For sandwiches/sliders, plan closer to 5 oz raw per adult. For mixed-protein buffets, about 8 oz raw.
