Questions and answers about corned beef brisket amounts

How much corned beef brisket per person?
As a main dish, plan about 7 ounces cooked per adult (≈5.5 oz teen, ≈4 oz child). For a mixed buffet, about 5 ounces cooked per adult. Appetizers are about 3 ounces cooked per adult. We convert cooked ounces to raw pounds using the cut’s shrink: flat ≈ 33 percent; point ≈ 40 percent.
What’s the difference between flat and point cut?
Flat is leaner and typically shrinks about one-third when cooked. Point is fattier and shrinks a little more (around two-fifths).
How do you calculate cost?
Subtotal = rounded raw pounds × unit price. We then add sales tax and any extra fees (e.g., delivery) to show an estimated total and per-person cost.

Quick notes

  • Per-adult cooked baselines: Main ≈ 7 oz; Mixed buffet ≈ 5 oz; Appetizer ≈ 3 oz. Teens ≈ 0.8 adult; Children ≈ 0.6 adult.
  • Shrink factors: Flat ≈ 33% loss (yield ≈ 67%); Point ≈ 40% loss (yield ≈ 60%).
  • Rounding: We round raw purchase to the nearest quarter-pound for easy shopping.
  • Context multipliers: Appetite, event length, and leftovers can shift amounts by ~10–20%.
  • Disclaimer: Estimates only; confirm with your butcher and adjust for your crowd.
