FAQs

How much spiral ham per person?
As a main dish, plan about 8 ounces cooked per adult (about 6.5 oz for teens and 4.8 oz for children). For a mixed buffet, plan about 6 ounces cooked per adult. Appetizers are about 3.5 ounces cooked per adult.
Bone-in vs. boneless — does it change how much to buy?
Yes — bone and trim affect yield. We estimate raw pounds by dividing cooked ounces by a style yield: Bone-in ≈ 60% yield (about 40% shrink) and Boneless ≈ 80% yield (about 20% shrink).
How do you convert pounds to about how many hams?
Enter Ham size each (lb). We divide rounded raw pounds by that number to get an about count of hams.
How much should I buy for 10, 25, 50, or 100 people?
For a main with typical appetite and bone-in spiral, rough guides are: 10 people ≈ 8–9 lb raw, 25 ≈ 20–23 lb, 50 ≈ 40–46 lb, 100 ≈ 80–92 lb. Use the calculator for your exact mix and settings.
How is pricing calculated?
Subtotal is rounded raw pounds × price per lb. Then we add your tax and any fees to estimate a total and per-person cost.

Quick notes

  • Per-adult cooked baselines: Main about 8 oz; Mixed about 6 oz; Appetizer about 3.5 oz. Teens ≈ 0.8 adult; Children ≈ 0.6 adult.
  • Yield by style (cooked/raw): Bone-in ≈ 0.60; Boneless ≈ 0.80.
  • Count: About hams = rounded raw pounds ÷ ham size each (lb).
  • Context multipliers: Appetite (0.90 / 1.00 / 1.20); Event length (0.95 / 1.00 / 1.08 / 1.15); Leftovers (No / +10% / +20%).
  • Rounding: Raw purchase rounded to the nearest quarter-pound for easy shopping.
