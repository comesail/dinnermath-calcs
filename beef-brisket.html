FAQs

How much raw brisket per person?
Plan ¾ lb raw per adult for a main dish, adjusting for teens and kids. This accounts for shrinkage so cooked portions land around ½ lb each.
What’s the shrinkage rate when cooking?
Corned beef brisket typically loses 30–35% of its raw weight during simmering or slow cooking. Point cuts lose slightly more due to fat content.
Flat cut vs point cut—what’s the difference?
Flat cut is leaner, easier to slice, and shrinks a bit less. Point cut has more marbling and flavor but cooks down more.
Can brisket be served in sliders or sandwiches?
Yes, for sandwiches or sliders use about ⅓ lb raw brisket per person, yielding ~2–3 ounces cooked each.
What if I’m serving brisket with other meats?
Use the mixed proteins mode: about ½ lb raw brisket per person. Combine with chicken, sausage, or other mains as part of the menu.
