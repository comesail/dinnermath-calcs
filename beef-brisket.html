FAQs

How many pounds of brisket per person?
Plan about 0.5 lb cooked per adult-equivalent. With ~60% cooked yield, that’s about 0.8–0.85 lb raw per adult-equivalent.
Flat vs point vs packer?
Flats are leaner (slightly higher yield), points are fattier (lower yield). Packer briskets vary based on how much you trim. Choose the yield option that best matches.
Buffet vs sandwiches?
For plated mains keep 0.5 lb cooked per adult-equivalent. For sandwiches you can reduce slightly if there are many sides, or add a leftovers buffer for seconds.
Should I round up?
Yes—brisket varies by trim and cook. Rounding up avoids running short.
