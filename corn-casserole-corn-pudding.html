FAQs

How much corn casserole per person?
As a side dish, plan about 6 oz per adult, about 5 oz per teen, and about 3.5 oz per child (before baking). Mixed buffets run lighter; vegetarian mains run heavier.
What do the pan-size labels mean?
Typical yields: 8x8 about 6 side servings (about 2.0 lb raw mix), 9x13 about 12 (about 4.0 lb), Deep 9x13 about 16 (about 5.5 lb), Half sheet 12x18 about 24 (about 8.0 lb). We convert your crowd to pans and raw pounds using the chosen size.
Side, mixed buffet, or vegetarian main — what changes?
The Course mode adjusts portions: Side (1.00x), Mixed buffet (0.75x), Vegetarian main (1.40x), Appetizer tasting (0.50x). Combine with appetite, event length, and leftovers to fit your crowd.
How does pricing work here?
Subtotal = rounded raw pounds × unit price per pound. Then we add your tax and any extra fees to estimate a total and per-person cost. Use your average ingredient cost per pound for the recipe (corn + dairy + mix-ins).
Can I switch pans after planning?
Yes. Change the pan dropdown to see about how many pans of each size you’d need for the same crowd and settings.

Quick notes

  • Base portions (raw mix): Adults about 6 oz; Teens about 5 oz; Children about 3.5 oz.
  • Course multipliers: Side 1.00x; Mixed 0.75x; Vegetarian main 1.40x; Appetizer 0.50x.
  • Pan yields (approx): 8x8 about 2.0 lb; 9x13 about 4.0 lb; Deep 9x13 about 5.5 lb; Half sheet about 8.0 lb.
  • Context multipliers: Appetite (0.90 / 1.00 / 1.20); Event length (0.95 / 1.00 / 1.08 / 1.15); Leftovers (No / +10% / +20%).
